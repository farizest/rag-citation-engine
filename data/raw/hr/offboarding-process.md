# Offboarding Process

**Owner:** People Team / IT Operations
**Last updated:** 2025-11-01
**Applies to:** All departing employees (voluntary or involuntary)

## Timeline overview

Offboarding is coordinated jointly by People Team and IT Operations, with
the process kicked off as soon as a departure date is confirmed —
ideally at least 2 weeks before last day for voluntary departures, though
this isn't always possible.

## Access revocation

All system access is revoked at end of day on the employee's last day,
not before — departing employees retain normal access through their
final day to allow proper handoff. The single exception is for
involuntary departures flagged as high-risk by People Team or Legal, in
which case access may be revoked immediately upon notification, with
manager and IT coordinating in real time. See Access Request Process for
how access was originally granted.

## Equipment return

Northwind-issued laptops and any other hardware (badges, hardware lab
equipment if applicable) must be returned within 5 business days of the
last day. For remote employees, IT provides a prepaid shipping label.
Unreturned equipment after 30 days is escalated to People Team for
follow-up per the equipment agreement signed at provisioning — see
Laptop Provisioning Process.

## Final paycheck and PTO

Because Northwind uses a flexible (non-accrual) PTO model — see Leave
and PTO Policy — there is no PTO payout calculation in most states/
regions. A small number of jurisdictions legally require accrued-PTO
payout regardless of company policy; Finance handles this automatically
based on work location, no action needed from the employee or manager.

## Exit interview

People Team schedules an optional exit interview, typically in the final
week. See Exit Interview Process for what's covered. This is voluntary
but strongly encouraged — feedback from exit interviews is one of the
more reliable signals we have for catching team-level or process issues
that don't otherwise surface.
