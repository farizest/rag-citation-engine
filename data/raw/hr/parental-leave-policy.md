# Parental Leave Policy

**Owner:** People Team
**Last updated:** 2025-09-01
**Applies to:** All full-time employees, regardless of tenure

## Summary

Northwind Robotics provides 16 weeks of fully paid parental leave for the
primary caregiver and 8 weeks of fully paid leave for a secondary
caregiver, available for birth, adoption, or fostering of a child. This
policy applies from your first day of employment — there is no minimum
tenure requirement, which is a deliberate departure from many
industry-standard policies that require 6-12 months of tenure first.

## Primary vs. secondary caregiver

The "primary" and "secondary" designation is about caregiving role, not
gender or legal parent status, and is self-identified by the employee(s).
In two-parent households where both parents work at Northwind, both may
take leave concurrently or staggered, and the household can choose how to
split the primary/secondary designation between them (it does not have to
be split 16/8 — for example, both partners could each take 12 weeks if
they coordinate with HR in advance, as long as total combined leave does
not exceed 24 weeks).

## Taking leave

- Leave does not need to be taken in one continuous block. With manager
  and HR coordination, it can be split (e.g. 10 weeks immediately
  following birth, then 6 weeks taken later in the child's first year).
- Leave must be taken within the first 12 months following the
  qualifying event.
- During leave, your role, level, and compensation band are fully
  protected. Benefits (including health insurance) continue uninterrupted
  — see Benefits Overview: Health Insurance.

## Returning to work

We offer an optional gradual return: up to 4 weeks at 50% time (full pay)
immediately following the end of formal leave, for employees who want a
transition period. This needs to be arranged with your manager and HR at
least 2 weeks before your planned return date.

## Coordination with PTO

Parental leave is separate from and does not draw down your general PTO
under the Leave and PTO Policy. You are not expected to "save up" PTO in
anticipation of a parental leave event.
