# Promotion Process

**Owner:** People Team
**Last updated:** 2025-10-15
**Applies to:** All full-time employees

## When promotions happen

Promotions are decided during the June and December performance review
cycles (see Performance Review Process) — there is no separate,
standalone promotion cycle. In exceptional cases (e.g. someone is
clearly and urgently operating at the next level and waiting for the
formal cycle would create a retention risk), an off-cycle promotion can
be requested by a manager with VP approval, but this is rare and meant
to be the exception.

## Nomination

Your manager nominates you for promotion ahead of the calibration week.
A promotion nomination requires a written packet: a summary of your
impact at the target level, typically referencing 2-3 specific projects
or initiatives, plus supporting peer feedback. For IC promotions to L5
and above, and all manager promotions, the packet also goes through a
cross-functional promotion committee, not just your manager and skip-level.

## Criteria

Promotion criteria are level-specific and documented in the leveling
guide (linked from the People Team wiki home). In general terms, what
matters most is sustained demonstration of next-level scope and impact
over multiple review cycles — a single great project is rarely sufficient
on its own. The most common reason a promotion packet doesn't succeed in
a given cycle is insufficient evidence of *sustained* (not one-time)
next-level performance, not a lack of any qualifying work.

## Outcome and timing

Promotion decisions are communicated during your performance review
conversation, alongside your rating. If approved, the compensation
adjustment associated with the promotion is reflected in the same payroll
cycle as the merit increase — see Compensation Bands Overview.

If a promotion is not approved in a given cycle, your manager is expected
to give specific, written feedback on what's needed for the next attempt,
not just "try again next cycle."
