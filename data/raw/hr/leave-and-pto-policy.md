# Leave and PTO Policy

**Owner:** People Team
**Last updated:** 2026-01-15
**Applies to:** All full-time employees

## Summary

Northwind Robotics uses a flexible PTO model for salaried employees rather
than an accrual system. There is no fixed annual day count — instead, we
ask that you take the time you need, coordinate with your manager and team
to ensure coverage, and aim for at least 15 days off per year as a
general guideline. Hourly and warehouse-operations staff are on a
separate accrual-based system described in Appendix B of the full
Employee Handbook.

## How to request time off

1. Submit your request through the HR portal at least 5 business days in
   advance for anything longer than 2 consecutive days. Shorter requests
   can be informal (a Slack message to your manager is fine), but should
   still be logged in the portal for payroll/coverage tracking.
2. Your manager approves or discusses scheduling conflicts. Approval is
   not meant to be a high bar — the main thing managers check for is team
   coverage during critical periods (e.g. don't take the same week as your
   teammate if you're both needed for an on-call rotation).
3. Once approved, the time is automatically reflected on the shared team
   calendar.

## Holidays

Northwind observes 10 paid company holidays per year, published at the
start of each calendar year on the People Team wiki space. Employees in
regions with different statutory holidays may swap a company holiday for
a locally relevant one with manager approval.

## Sick leave

Sick leave is separate from PTO and is not capped — if you're unwell,
stay home and recover. There's no requirement to provide documentation for
absences under 3 consecutive days. For absences of 3+ consecutive days,
HR may request a note from a healthcare provider, primarily for
short-term disability coordination purposes, not as a trust issue.

## Unused time off

Because this is a flexible (not accrual-based) policy, there is no
"unused PTO payout" upon departure — see Offboarding Process for what
final paychecks do and don't include. We strongly encourage managers to
proactively flag to their reports if it looks like they haven't taken
meaningful time off in a while; sustained zero-PTO patterns are something
managers are asked to address in 1:1s, not something to be quietly proud
of.
