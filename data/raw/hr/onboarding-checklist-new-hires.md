# Onboarding Checklist: New Hires

**Owner:** People Team
**Last updated:** 2026-01-10
**Audience:** New employees and their managers

## Before day one

- HR sends an offer confirmation and start date email, including a link
  to the benefits enrollment portal — see Benefits Overview: Health
  Insurance and Benefits Overview: Retirement (401k) for what you'll be
  choosing between.
- IT provisions your laptop and ships it to your home address (remote/
  hybrid hires) or has it waiting at your desk (office-first roles). See
  Laptop Provisioning Process — this typically takes 5-7 business days,
  so it's triggered as soon as your start date is confirmed, not on day
  one.
- Your manager should have a written 30/60/90 day plan drafted before you
  start — this isn't optional, People Team checks for it during the new
  hire's first week.

## Day one

1. IT helpdesk session (in person or virtual) to activate your accounts,
   set up multi-factor authentication, and walk through initial VPN
   configuration. Full detail in VPN Setup Guide and Password and MFA
   Policy.
2. Read the Code of Conduct and Company Mission and Values — both are
   required reading and acknowledged via a checkbox in the HR portal.
3. Meet with your manager to review the 30/60/90 day plan.
4. Optional but encouraged: block 30 minutes to read the System
   Architecture Overview (engineering hires) or relevant department
   overview docs, to get oriented before diving into specific tasks.

## First week

- Complete mandatory compliance training: anti-harassment policy
  acknowledgment, data classification policy training, and (for
  engineering roles) security vulnerability disclosure process training.
- Get added to relevant Slack channels — your manager should walk you
  through which ones, but `#all-hands`, your team channel, and
  `#it-helpdesk` are universal.
- Schedule 1:1s with immediate teammates. Managers: see Manager
  Resources: 1:1 Guide for how to structure these.

## First 30 days

- Submit any access requests for systems beyond your default access — see
  Access Request Process. Most new hires need at least one additional
  access request beyond what's auto-provisioned (e.g. a specific
  warehouse's telemetry data, a customer account in the CRM).
- Complete your first 1:1 with your skip-level manager.

## Notes for managers

The biggest onboarding failure mode we see is access requests not being
submitted until the new hire is already blocked on day 3 or 4. Submit
known access requests during the "before day one" phase wherever possible
— see Access Request Process for what can be pre-approved.
