# Compensation Bands Overview

**Owner:** People Team / Finance
**Last updated:** 2026-01-01
**Applies to:** All full-time employees

## How bands work

Every role at Northwind maps to a level (L1 through L7 for individual
contributors, M1 through M5 for managers) and a band, which is a salary
range for that level in a given geographic market. Bands are reviewed
annually each January using third-party market compensation survey data.

Bands are intentionally wide (typically 30-40% from minimum to maximum)
to account for experience and demonstrated impact within a level, not
just tenure. Being at the top vs. bottom of your band is not itself a
performance signal — someone can be a strong performer and still be
relatively early in their band if they're newer to the level.

## Geographic differentiation

Northwind uses a 3-tier geographic system (Tier 1: major metro hubs like
the Bay Area and Seattle; Tier 2: other major cities; Tier 3: lower
cost-of-living regions) rather than fully localized pay-by-city. Moving
between tiers (e.g. relocating, or shifting to fully remote in a
different tier) can trigger a band adjustment — see Relocation Assistance
Policy for the process.

## How pay changes happen

Pay can change for three reasons:
1. **Annual band adjustment** — if market data shifts a band, anyone
   below the new minimum is adjusted up; this is not tied to individual
   performance.
2. **Merit increase** — informed by your Performance Review Process
   rating, applied during the December cycle, effective each January.
3. **Promotion** — moving to a new level typically includes a
   compensation adjustment to be appropriately positioned in the new
   band. See Promotion Process for criteria.

## Equity

Most roles include an equity component (RSUs, vesting over 4 years with
a 1-year cliff). Equity refresh grants are considered as part of the
annual cycle for strong, sustained performers, not given automatically
every year.
