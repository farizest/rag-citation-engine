# Expense Reimbursement Policy

**Owner:** Finance
**Last updated:** 2026-01-12
**Applies to:** All employees

## General principle

Spend Northwind's money like it's your own: reasonable, necessary for
the work, and something you'd be comfortable explaining to your manager.
We don't maintain an exhaustive list of pre-approved vs. banned expense
categories — judgment is expected, and Finance will flag anything that
seems out of line during review rather than requiring pre-approval for
every category.

## Submission process

1. Submit expenses through the Finance portal within 30 days of the
   expense. Expenses submitted after 60 days require manager
   justification and may be denied at Finance's discretion.
2. Receipts are required for any single expense over $25. Below that
   threshold, a description is sufficient.
3. Your manager approves the expense report; Finance does a secondary
   review for policy compliance and processes reimbursement, typically
   within 5 business days of manager approval.

## Common categories

- **Meals**: reasonable team meals and client meals are covered. Daily
  meal limits while traveling follow the per-diem guidance in Travel
  Booking Policy.
- **Home office equipment**: a one-time $500 allowance for remote/hybrid
  employees to set up a home office (desk, chair, monitor), available
  once per employment unless there's a documented ergonomic need for
  additional equipment.
- **Professional development**: courses, conferences, and books directly
  relevant to your role, up to $1,500/year without additional approval
  beyond your manager; above that requires a brief written justification.
- **Travel**: covered in detail in Travel Booking Policy, including
  flight class guidelines and lodging norms.

## What's not covered

- Personal entertainment unrelated to business purpose
- Commuting costs to your regular office (this is considered a personal
  expense, not a business one, consistent with standard practice)
- Alcohol beyond a reasonable amount with client or team meals
